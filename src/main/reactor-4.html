Reactor 4 – Chernobyl

Reactor 4 at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant was the site of the catastrophic explosion on April 26, 1986 — the worst nuclear disaster in history. A combination of flawed reactor design and operator error led to a massive release of radioactive material, forcing mass evacuations and creating an exclusion zone that remains active to this day.

Today, guided tours to the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone — including a controlled view of Reactor 4's sarcophagus — are legally allowed. Visitors must follow strict safety protocols and stay within designated paths. Despite the risks, it's one of the most unique and haunting destinations in the world.
