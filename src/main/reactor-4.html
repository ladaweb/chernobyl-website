Reactor 4 – Chernobyl

Reactor 4 at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant was the site of the catastrophic explosion on April 26, 1986 — the worst nuclear disaster in history. A flawed reactor design and operator error caused a massive release of radioactive material, which forced the evacuation of over 100,000 people and left a lasting environmental impact.

Radiation Level:

1.2 µSv/h

(µSv/h = microsieverts per hour)

🕐 Approximate Safe Exposure Time: ~833 hours/year

Despite the risks, guided tours to the Exclusion Zone — including a distant view of Reactor 4's sarcophagus — are legally permitted. Visitors must follow strict safety guidelines and avoid high-dose zones. Radiation levels are monitored and brief exposure is considered safe.
